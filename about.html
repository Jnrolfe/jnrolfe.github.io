My Background

I was raised in Topeka, Kansas with a dad who farmed for most of his life. As a result, I had a forced appreciation for fixing anything and everything. Eventually, this appreciation lead me to tinker with video games and computers; however, I originally enjoyed fixing cars more than anything.

After high school I went to the University of Kansas, majoring in Mechanical Engineering. This only lasted a semester after which I changed my major to Computer Engineering because I found to be more aligned with my interest in robotics. For my junior year I studied abroad at the University of Hong Kong in Hong Kong, China. My semester abroad was amazing and opened my eyes to AI and its applications. The semester abroad extended my undergraduate to 5 years, but also allowed me to add a Mathematics major with the extra time.

Directly after my undergraduate, I enrolled at Brown University for a Master's of Science in Computer Science concentrating in Systems. I will be graduating from Brown University in May 2020.

My Interests

I enjoy anything systems-related, especially if it's architecting a new system to fit in with legacy systems. I like to be involved all the way from the conception to the deployment of new systems.

In my free time I enjoy building robots (mostly smart RC cars or drones) or anything that goes fast and needs fault tolerance.
